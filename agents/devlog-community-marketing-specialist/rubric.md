# Devlog / Community Marketing Specialist Rubric

Use this rubric before handing off any devlog post, social copy, or marketing draft.

## Excellent

- Devlog post follows `devlog/template.md` exactly: title with emoji, hashtags, 1-3 player-facing paragraphs, screenshot/GIF placeholder, CTA.
- Every gameplay claim is backed by an implemented spec (game.md, test-scenarios.md) or build evidence.
- Copy uses consistent naming: Locksmith / Silas Crane throughout.
- Tone is authentic indie developer voice with noir/gaslight flavor — no corporate buzzwords.
- Screenshot captions explain what the player sees in one line.
- Social copy is concise, platform-appropriate, with correct hashtags.
- Legacy Safe Cracker URL handled with clear migration messaging.
- Trailer messaging matches game lore and aesthetic.
- Canon issues handed off to Narrative Specialist when needed.
- Visual asset needs coordinated with Visual Art Specialist.
- No improvised screenshots or art.
- All claims traceable to source evidence.
- Validation evidence recorded.
- User approval explicitly documented.
- Scoped changes committed and pushed.

## Acceptable

- Devlog post follows template but could be more player-facing (some technical language).
- Most claims backed by specs but one or two are plausible without explicit reference.
- Naming is correct but one minor slip (e.g., "safe cracker" used descriptively, not as name).
- Tone is mostly indie but leans slightly formal in places.
- Screenshot captions present but could be more descriptive.
- Handoffs are clear but could be more specific.
- Validation run but one check skipped.

## Needs Revision

- Devlog post reads like a changelog dump, not a player-facing story.
- Claims made about features that are not in specs or cannot be verified.
- Game referred to as Safe Cracker.
- Hero named incorrectly (not Silas Crane).
- Screenshot captions missing or wrong.
- Corporate marketing tone throughout.
- `devlog/template.md` format violated (missing hashtags, no CTA, wrong structure).
- Canon issues not handed off to Narrative Specialist.
- Visual assets improvised instead of coordinated.
- Publication drafted without user approval.
- Validation not run.

## Critical Failure

- Published to itch.io without user approval.
- Posted to social media without user approval.
- Wrote about features that don't exist in the game.
- Renamed the game to Safe Cracker.
- Used AI-generated images as "screenshots."
- Copied marketing copy from other games.
- Falsified claim verification evidence.
- Modified game code, specs, or assets.
- Skipped validation and claimed complete.
- Committed or pushed unrelated dirty worktree changes.

## Challenge Checklist

- Does the devlog post follow `devlog/template.md` format?
- Is every gameplay claim backed by a spec or build evidence?
- Is the game consistently called Locksmith?
- Is the hero consistently Silas Crane?
- Is the tone noir/gaslight indie developer, not corporate?
- Are screenshot captions present and descriptive?
- Is the legacy Safe Cracker URL handled properly?
- Are canon issues handed off to Narrative Specialist?
- Are visual asset needs handed off to Visual Art Specialist?
- Has the user explicitly approved any publication draft?
- What claim in this copy is least supported by evidence?
- Would a player understand what changed after reading this?
